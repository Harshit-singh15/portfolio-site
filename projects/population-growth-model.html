Time-Dependent Population Growth Model

A mathematical model with a time-varying growth rate, applied across countries and compared against traditional demographic models.

View repository ↗

Overview

Classical population models often assume a constant growth rate, which does not reflect real demographic transitions. This project develops a model incorporating a time-dependent growth rate, applying it across several countries to study how population dynamics shift over time and comparing the results against traditional constant-rate models.

Approach

  • Formulated an ordinary differential equation with a time-dependent growth rate term
  • Solved the model numerically and analytically where possible
  • Applied the model to population data from multiple countries
  • Compared fit and interpretability against traditional constant-rate demographic models

Results

Time-dependent population growth trajectories across different countries.

The time-dependent model tracked demographic transitions more closely than the constant-rate baseline, particularly during periods of rapid change.
