Predictive Analytics for TB Treatment Outcomes

Machine learning and neural network models predicting tuberculosis treatment outcomes from patient and facility-level data.

View repository ↗

Overview

Predicting which tuberculosis patients are at risk of poor treatment outcomes can help clinicians intervene earlier. This project develops and benchmarks several model families — Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, SVM, and neural networks — on patient-level clinical and facility data to predict treatment outcomes.

Feature importance analysis was used throughout to keep the models interpretable and to surface the clinical factors most associated with high-risk profiles.

Approach

  • Cleaned and prepared patient-level clinical and facility datasets
  • Trained and benchmarked five model families on the same prediction task
  • Ran feature importance analysis to identify key predictors of poor outcomes
  • Compared models on performance and interpretability to select the most reliable approach

Results

Performance comparison of different machine learning models for predicting TB treatment outcomes.

Benchmarking identified the model family with the best balance of predictive performance and interpretability for this dataset.
